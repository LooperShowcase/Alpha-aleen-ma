Pagani Huayra

About Pagani Huayra:



Information:

The Pagani Huayra is a mid-engine sports car produced by Italian sportcar manufacturer Pagani. It is named after Wayra Tata, the Quechua wind god. The Huayra was named "The Hypercar of the Year 2012".

The top speed of "Pagani Huayra"

The Huayra has a top speed of about 383 km/h (238 mph) and it has a 0-97 km/h (60 mph) acceleration time of 2.8 seconds. Using Pirelli tyres, the Pagani Huayra is capable of 1.66 g of lateral acceleration.

What Engine Does Pagani Huayra use?

The Huayra uses a 6.0 L twin-turbocharged M158 60° V12 engine developed by Mercedes-AMG specially for the Huayra, which has a power output of 740 PS at 5,800 rpm and 1,000 N⋅m of torque at 2,250-4,500 rpm. The engine has been designed at the request of Pagani to reduce turbo lag and improve response, achieved with smaller turbochargers, a different intercooler configuration and re-programmed ECU settings.

What Countries Allow the Pagani Huayra To be driven On The Road?

The Standard Pagani Huayra is road-legal in most countries, while the Huayra R needs modifications To Be Street Legal.

Why Is The Pagani Huayra So Expensive?

Pagani vehicles are all handmade, and they use the latest technologies and materials to create some of the most beautiful and exclusive cars in the world. Every detail of a Pagani car is carefully considered, and no expense is spared in order to create a truly luxurious experience.
